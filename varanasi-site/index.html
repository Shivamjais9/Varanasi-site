Manikarnika Ghat

Famous For: Sacred cremation rituals symbolizing Moksha (liberation).

Manikarnika Ghat is one of the oldest and most sacred ghats in Varanasi. It is believed that anyone cremated here attains Moksha ‚Äî freedom from the cycle of life and death. The ghat never sleeps, with the sacred fire burning continuously, representing the eternal soul.

Best Time: Early morning or evening, when the rituals create a deeply spiritual atmosphere.

Tulsi Manas Mandir

Dedicated To: Lord Rama

The temple marks the place where saint Tulsidas wrote the epic Ramcharitmanas. Its walls are inscribed with verses from the holy text.

Ramnagar Fort

Built In: 18th century by Raja Balwant Singh

Located across the Ganga, this sandstone fort showcases royal artefacts, vintage cars, and a grand view of the riverfront.

Varanasi, also known as Kashi or Banaras, is one of the oldest living cities in the world. Situated on the banks of the holy Ganga, it represents the spiritual heart of India ‚Äî where devotion, culture, and eternity coexist.
